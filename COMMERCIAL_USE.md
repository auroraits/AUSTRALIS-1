# Commercial use

AUSTRALIS-1 / DIY-Nanosat is public for education, personal experimentation,
non-commercial research and non-commercial open collaboration.

The public license does not authorize commercial exploitation.

## Commercial use requires permission

Contact the project owner before using this repository for:

- paid products, paid services, consulting deliverables, contract R&D or client work;
- manufacturing, selling, licensing, sublicensing or distributing derived hardware;
- integrating the designs, datasets, software or mission architecture into a commercial product;
- training, fine-tuning, benchmarking or validating a commercial model or commercial service;
- using the AUSTRALIS-1 name, Aurora ITS name, mission identity, branding, logos or confusingly similar marks;
- filing patents or other exclusive rights based on this work.

## Why this restriction exists

The goal is to share knowledge and let the community learn, reproduce, critique
and improve the work without allowing a third party to capture the project
commercially.

Copyright and public licenses do not fully protect ideas, discoveries, functional
hardware concepts or inventions. For important inventions or brand protection,
separate patent, trademark or contractual strategy may be required before broad
publication.

## Current enforcement posture

The project intends to use a clean public mirror plus dual commercial licensing.
The public repository is for non-commercial use only. Commercial users must get
a separate written license that defines the permitted field, manufacturing
rights, flight/launch permissions, support expectations, attribution, fees,
patent scope, termination and governing law.

Before accepting external contributions, the project also needs an inbound
contributor license workflow so accepted contributions can be used and
commercially licensed by the project owner.

See `LEGAL_ENFORCEMENT_REVIEW.md` and `CONTRIBUTING.md`.
